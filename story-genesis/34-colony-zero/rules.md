=== RULES ===
# Rules: Colony Zero

## Setting

### Location & Time
- **Mars, Jezero Crater region**, approximately 2040s-2050s (exact year left flexible)
- Colony Zero: first permanent human settlement, 200 colonists, established 7 years prior
- Four pressurized habitat modules (Alpha through Delta) connected by sealed corridors
- Mining operations, greenhouses, water reclamation, medical bay, command center
- Communication delay to Earth: 4-24 minutes depending on orbital position

### Physical Constraints (MUST be respected)
- **Atmosphere**: 95% CO2, ~0.6% surface pressure. Unprotected exposure = death in ~90 seconds
- **Temperature**: Average -60C, range from -120C to +20C near equator at noon
- **Gravity**: 0.38g (affects movement, long-term bone/muscle health, fluid dynamics in body)
- **Radiation**: No global magnetic field. Surface radiation ~0.67 mSv/day (chronic exposure concern)
- **Dust**: Fine iron oxide particles, pervasive, damaging to lungs and equipment
- **Light**: Sun appears ~60% the size seen from Earth. Thin atmosphere = short twilights, pale sky
- **Sound**: Almost no sound transmission outdoors (atmosphere too thin). Sound carries through ground/structure contact.

### Colony Infrastructure
- Aging life support (HEPA filters, CO2 scrubbers, water reclamation) all past design life
- EVA suits used hundreds of times beyond rated lifespan
- Greenhouses produce limited fresh food (primarily lettuce, some other hydroponics)
- Power: solar arrays + small nuclear RTGs
- No return vehicle exists. Colony was designed as one-way.
- Last resupply arrived 11 months late with only 60% payload. Next resupply never launched.

### The Artifact
- Located 40 meters beneath surface, ~800 meters east of colony
- Made of platinum-group metals (iridium, osmium, rhenium) with impossible 50-50 isotopic ratios
- Material manufactured at atomic level -- beyond any known human or natural process
- Constant 22.0C surface temperature (later rising), self-sustaining internal atmosphere (Earth-standard air)
- Covered in geometric patterns that pulse with amber luminescence
- Contains internal chambers, including a central room with a pillar displaying images
- Minimum age: 150 million years. Possibly 500+ million years or older.
- Activated by human touch (skin contact with surface)
- Displays images of Earth's geological history and apparent future
- Emits a tone (starting ~136.1 Hz, ascending) that travels through ground, not air
- Spreading: luminescent pattern lines extending across Martian surface from dig site
- Has displayed text on colony electronics: "WE HAVE BEEN WAITING"

### Earth Status
- Communications fully severed. No signal on any frequency. No emergency beacons, no background radio noise.
- Occurred simultaneously with artifact's escalation phase
- Cause unknown. Colony has no way to investigate.

## Characters

### Commander Adaeze Obi (Primary POV)
- **Background**: Nigerian-born engineer, 47. Former NASRDA (Nigerian Space Research and Development Agency). 12 years navigating bureaucratic politics before colony recruitment.
- **Traits**: Calm under pressure, politically astute, pragmatic. Leads by competence, not charisma. Does not belong to either political faction. Carries the weight of 200 lives as a physical sensation.
- **Physical**: Close-cropped gray hair, deep brown skin, fine lines from UV exposure. Moves with deliberate economy.
- **Voice**: Precise, measured. Uses questions strategically. Shows nothing while processing everything.
- **Arc direction**: Must evolve from colony manager to leader of an isolated civilization facing the unknown.

### Dr. Ravi Patel (Secondary POV)
- **Background**: Chennai-born geologist (areologist), 34. Adapted to Mars gravity better than anyone. Seven years have made him a polymath by necessity.
- **Traits**: Preternatural calm, deep analytical mind, always running calculations three layers deeper than the conversation. Keeps a personal voice recorder (graduate school habit). Meticulous about protocol.
- **Physical**: Lean, angular frame. Moves fluidly in low-g.
- **Voice**: Technical, precise, but capable of wonder. When shaken, goes quieter, not louder.
- **Arc direction**: The scientist forced to confront something beyond the boundaries of science. His methodical worldview is his greatest strength and his greatest vulnerability.

### Dr. Yuki Tanaka (Secondary POV)
- **Background**: Osaka-born physician, ~45-50. Trained during pandemic years. Chose Mars for intellectual challenge, not romance.
- **Traits**: Clinical, narrow emotional register (by disposition, not suppression). Unflinching diagnostic clarity. Privately carrying her own medical crisis.
- **Secret**: Developing progressive tremor in left hand. Differential diagnosis not yet confirmed. Colony's only physician cannot treat herself with available supplies.
- **Physical**: Small, precise. Hair in a permanent knot. Presses her trembling hand flat to suppress it.
- **Voice**: Clinical vocabulary even in internal monologue. Dry, understated.
- **Arc direction**: Must navigate being the colony's healer while becoming its patient. Her clinical detachment will be tested by phenomena that defy clinical frameworks.

### Karl Fischer (Antagonist/Complicating Force)
- **Background**: German systems analyst. Arrived crew rotation two. Leader of the Returnist faction.
- **Traits**: Intelligent, charismatic, principled in his way. Mistakes certainty for intelligence. Political animal. Not evil -- genuinely concerned for colonists, but pursues power as the means.
- **Role**: Political opposition to Adaeze. Represents the human impulse to retreat from the unknown. His arguments are always superficially sound and practically problematic.

### Supporting Cast
- **Priya Sharma**: Communications officer. From Jaipur. Perpetually strained. Knows what day it is, Earth-relative.
- **Tomasz Kowalski**: Life support engineer. Tall Pole. Haunted eyes. Systems thinker who defaults to worst-case analysis.
- **Liu Wei**: Agriculturalist. Protective of greenhouses. Pragmatic voice of reason.
- **Sasha Volkov**: Geochemist, 26. Sergei's granddaughter. Moscow-born. Youngest science team member. Most susceptible to the artifact's patterns.
- **Kwame Asante**: Structural engineer, 31. Strong EVA worker. History of knee inflammation. Reliable under pressure.
- **Amara Diallo**: Colony council chair. Senegalese atmospheric scientist. Diplomatic instincts.
- **Jin-ho Park**: Mining specialist, 40. Seoul-born. Elevated intracranial pressure (medical concern).

## Story Rules

### Narrative Constraints
1. **Hard SF**: All Mars science must be accurate. No artificial gravity, no faster-than-light communication, no magic technology (the artifact is the sole exception, and its properties must be internally consistent).
2. **Multiple POV**: Rotate between Adaeze, Ravi, and Yuki. Each has a distinct voice and cognitive style. Adaeze thinks in strategic/political terms. Ravi thinks in scientific/analytical terms. Yuki thinks in clinical/diagnostic terms.
3. **No easy answers**: The artifact's nature and purpose should remain ambiguous and slowly revealed. Resist the urge to explain. Mystery deepens before it clarifies.
4. **Colony politics are real**: The Returnist/Rooter divide is not a background detail. It's a genuine ideological conflict that the discovery and the silence intensify. Fischer is not a strawman.
5. **Resource scarcity drives decisions**: Every choice has a cost. EVA hours, suit wear, medical supplies, food, water, power -- these are finite and shrinking. The artifact competes with survival for the colony's attention and resources.
6. **The silence is total**: Earth is completely gone. No partial signals, no degraded transmissions, no "almost heard something." The silence is absolute and remains so.
7. **The artifact is not friendly or hostile**: It is other. Its behavior may appear purposeful but should resist easy anthropomorphization. It is not a character with motives humans can understand.
8. **Physical reality**: Characters get tired, hungry, cold, sick, scared. Mars is not a backdrop -- it is an active antagonist. Dust, cold, radiation, and equipment failure are constant threats.
9. **Psychological realism**: 200 isolated humans under existential stress will exhibit the full range of human responses: courage, cowardice, brilliance, stupidity, kindness, cruelty. No one is purely heroic or purely villainous.
10. **Cliffhangers**: Every chapter must end on a moment of escalation, revelation, or dread.

### Tone
- Andy Weir's technical groundedness (show the math, show the engineering)
- Arkady Martine's political complexity (power, language, identity, colonialism)
- 2001: A Space Odyssey's cosmic awe (the alien is truly alien, vast, indifferent)
- Mounting dread that never fully resolves into action-movie catharsis

### Things to Avoid
- Alien communication that humans quickly decode
- Military/action solutions to cosmic-scale problems
- Love triangles or romantic subplots as primary drivers
- Characters who are defined solely by their expertise
- Exposition dumps (information emerges through character action and observation)
- Deus ex machina resolutions
- Making the artifact "evil" or "benevolent" -- it should be genuinely alien in its indifference

## Plot Direction

### Immediate (Chapters 4-6)
- Colony fractures politically as Fischer exploits the crisis
- Artifact continues to spread across Martian surface; luminescent lines reach colony perimeter
- Yuki's medical condition worsens; she must decide whether to disclose it
- Ravi enters the structure alone and discovers deeper chambers with more complex imagery
- The pillar displays a new image: not Earth, but Mars. Mars as it was billions of years ago -- with oceans, atmosphere, life
- The "WE HAVE BEEN WAITING" message reappears on multiple colony systems

### Medium-term (Chapters 7-12)
- The artifact's spreading patterns begin to interact with colony infrastructure (power fluctuations, life support anomalies -- but also improvements?)
- A faction forms that believes the artifact is beneficial and should be embraced
- Someone attempts to destroy or seal the artifact; the attempt fails in a way that reveals more about its nature
- The tone becomes a carrier wave: data is encoded in it, and Ravi begins to decode it
- The decoded data appears to be instructions -- but instructions for what?
- Evidence emerges that the artifact was not left by visitors but by something that lived on Mars

### Long-term (Chapters 13+)
- The artifact's purpose is gradually revealed: it is a seed. A biological/technological system designed to restore Mars to habitability, a process that was interrupted and is now resuming.
- The "silence from Earth" is connected: Earth's own artifact (buried deep, undiscovered) has activated simultaneously. The activation process temporarily disrupts electromagnetic systems on a planetary scale.
- The colonists must decide: cooperate with a process they don't fully understand that could make Mars livable but would transform it beyond recognition, or resist and maintain the Mars they know (dying, but theirs).
- The ultimate question is not "what is the artifact?" but "who gets to decide the future of a world?"
- Yuki's condition is connected to the artifact's biological component (the tremor is not a disease but an adaptation).
- The novel ends ambiguously: Mars is changing, Earth's signal returns but is different, and the colonists face a choice with no clear right answer.
