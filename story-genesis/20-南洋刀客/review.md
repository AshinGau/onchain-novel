**Round 1 Issues Identified:**
1. Opening needs a stronger hook — start with the emotional wound, not the calendar
2. Chapter 1 middle (lodging section) is too functional — needs atmospheric and emotional texture
3. Fight scenes over-explain technique names instead of letting action speak
4. Voice needs more distinctive Gu Long-style terse poetry — shorter sentences, more rhythm
5. Japanese ship subplot feels disconnected from main narrative
6. Need more multicultural texture — Malay/Indian characters beyond background decoration

### Final Assessment

**Strengths:**
- The "止" (stop/cease) philosophical thread gives the lone-warrior narrative genuine depth beyond typical wuxia
- Nanyang setting is fresh for the wuxia genre — multicultural martial arts encounters (Chinese kung fu vs. Muay Thai vs. Silat) feel natural rather than gimmicky
- Voice successfully blends Gu Long's terse lone-swordsman style with rich Nanyang sensory detail
- Strong supporting cast with clear motivations and distinctive traits
- The escalation from dock worker → night rescuer → arena champion is well-paced

**Open Threads for Continuation:**
1. The Japanese ship's mystery cargo — what was in the lighter boxes?
2. Zhong Younian's plan to challenge Lin Zhenbang from within Yihe Tang
3. Dapao's inevitable revenge attempt
4. Lin Haisheng's apprenticeship journey
5. Su Mingyuan — potential ally or future opponent?
6. The deeper meaning of "止" — Chen Sandao's philosophical quest
7. The乌水刀's backstory and previous owner
8. Colonial British authorities — how will they react to Chen's rising reputation?
9. Zhuang Tianci's shared history with Zhao Baichuan — what other secrets does he hold?
10. The rubber plantations and tin mines beyond the port city
