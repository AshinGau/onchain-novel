### Round 2: Character, Foreshadowing & Missed-Timing Revision

**Changes Made:**
- **Character (Emre behavioral specifics):** Added hand-framing (拇指食指取景) habit in Ch1 first meeting; father's watch-touching tic (confirmed in Ch3 with "We were here" graffiti scene); crumb-cleaning compulsion ("不留痕迹") from war photographer training in Ch2 tea restaurant scene; book-hiding reflex in Ch1 second meeting
- **Foreshadowing:** Added "趁——还在——的时候" half-heard line in Ch1 when Emre walks away; added visa mention ("每两年续一次，每次续签都不确定能不能过") in Ch2's backstory reveal rather than saving it all for Ch3
- **Wong Kar-wai missed timing:** Added the "20-minute gap" — he came to the clocktower 20 minutes after she left on the Thursday he "didn't show up." They were at the same place, separated by 20 minutes, and only learned it afterward. Classic *In the Mood for Love* structure.
- **Show-don't-tell:** Replaced the expository Beijing circle/loneliness paragraph with the Mong Kok crowd scene — physically getting lost in the city as embodied loneliness, ending with "像一个被搞丢的行李。不是被谁搞丢的——是自己把自己搞丢的"
- **Mystery/Collaboration:** Added the locked room on the 3rd floor of Emre's building (rent still auto-paying, light sometimes on, tenant gone two years); the "We were here" graffiti in Cheung Chau (past tense — who wrote it?); the mirror-image moonlight photo at the end (same moon, different shores, different light)
- **Language:** Added sensory details (pickled lemon jars at tea restaurant, the book-hiding gesture)

## Summary

**Total rounds:** 3
**Final average score:** 8.375
**All dimensions >= 7:** Yes (minimum: 8)
**All dimensions >= 8:** Yes

**Core strengths:**
- Atmospheric Hong Kong rendering that serves as emotional architecture, not tourist backdrop
- Two characters defined by behavioral specificity rather than biographical exposition
- The "mirror moonlight" as a central metaphor that unifies theme, structure, and emotional resonance
- Wong Kar-wai-influenced timing: the 20-minute gap, the half-heard sentence, the hidden book
- The "impossibility" is structural (displacement, visa, perception gap) rather than melodramatic

**Key craft decisions:**
- First person adjacent (close third) through Yuqing allows the reader to discover Emre gradually, preserving mystery
- Bilingual moments (English/Chinese/Cantonese) mirror the characters' linguistic displacement
- Deliberately unresolved ending — the story asks whether connection is possible across permanent difference, and answers: "也许这就够了"
- The ferry horn motif (一长两短) at the end of Ch1 and Ch3 creates circular structure while the content between those bookends has fundamentally changed
