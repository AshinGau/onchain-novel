### Round 2: Hook, Character & Subtext Revision

**Changes Made:**
- **Hook**: Replaced abstract rain opening with the "Bonjour" incident — Wanning discovers she instinctively says "Bonjour" to a Chinese supermarket cashier, establishing identity displacement as a concrete, startling event. "来巴黎整整四十三天，她的母语正在漏水" immediately grounds the theme.
- **Character (Théo)**: Added Vietnamese mother (revealed through Mireille, not Théo himself — "il ne parle jamais de sa mère"). Added the phone fight with "Papa" — Théo turning a page of Kafka back and forth "像在使用一种纸质的安眠药". Added the "Roman - v.14" detail — a novel rewritten fourteen times, explaining why he says "j'essaie d'écrire" instead of "j'écris". In Ch3, connected his Vietnamese heritage to his understanding of Wanning's Chinese — he too is a person between two languages.
- **Character (Supporting)**: Chen Ruoshui now has oil-scar "menu" on her forearm (宫保鸡丁/麻婆豆腐/干煸四季豆). Liao Mingxuan now has the "electricity on the tongue" scene — explaining "麻" to a horrified French neighbor.
- **World**: Added floor-by-floor smells in the apartment building (beurre brûlé, Arab spices, cat piss, nothing, old wood). Added Delacroix's cash-counting ritual. Added the fenêtre/窗户 phone call with mother — Wanning can't find the Chinese word for "window".
- **Subtext**: Trimmed Ch3 diary entry from 200+ words to 50 words. Removed explicit thematic statements ("有些东西只能存在于语言之外", "所有的漂泊都是为了找到一个值得停下来的人"). Replaced final ending with "吃过了" — a three-word callback to mother's "别忘了吃饭" that carries the entire emotional weight without explaining itself.
- **Callbacks**: Built structural echo between Ch1 opening ("Bonjour" to Cantonese cashier = language leaking) and Ch3 ending (French man mispronouncing her name = "最好听的发音"). "Not leaking — expanding."

## Final Assessment

**巴黎左岸的雨** is a three-chapter romance about Su Wanning, a 24-year-old Chinese comparative literature student at the Sorbonne, and Théo Renard, a 27-year-old French-Vietnamese aspiring novelist. Set on Paris's Left Bank during the autumn and winter months, the story uses rain as a structural and emotional motif — from the "Bonjour" incident that opens the novel to the "雨" character dissolving on a palm that marks its climax.

**Core Strengths:**
- The bilingual texture is the novel's defining feature — French dialogue woven into Chinese prose creates a reading experience that mirrors the protagonist's between-languages existence
- The "language leaking vs. language expanding" arc offers a fresh take on the immigrant experience
- Théo's Vietnamese-French heritage creates an unexpected symmetry — both leads are people living in the gap between languages
- The "吃过了" ending is powerful in its restraint — three characters that contain the entire emotional arc
- The café "Le Petit Naufrage" (Little Shipwreck) functions as a character and a metaphor without over-explanation
