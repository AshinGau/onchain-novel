# Rules: Blood Debt of Saigon

## Setting
- **Time**: Spans from the Fall of Saigon (April 1975) through the 1990s. The main narrative present is approximately 1997-1998, when Minh is nineteen and leaving Vietnam.
- **Primary locations**: District 4, Saigon (Ho Chi Minh City) -- narrow alleys, motorbike repair shops, pho carts, monsoon flooding. The Majestic Hotel in District 1. The library on Ly Tu Trong Street. A fabric warehouse in District 5 (formerly an ARVN intelligence office).
- **Destination**: Orange County, California -- Little Saigon, Bolsa Avenue, Saigon Plaza shopping center.
- **Atmosphere**: Postwar Vietnam navigating between memory and erasure. The war is "not history but a bruise everyone pressed against without meaning to." Pervasive concealment -- identities hidden, histories buried, collaborators punished. Smells of diesel, rot, charcoal smoke, jasmine, frying oil, and jackfruit.
- **Social context**: The children of ARVN collaborators live under suspicion. Overseas Vietnamese (Viet Kieu) return with American passports and complicated guilt. Vietnam is still learning to reconcile its past with its present.

## Characters
- **Minh** (born ~1974-1975): Protagonist. Son of Tran Van Khanh. Raised by Ba Tam in District 4 without knowing his true parentage. Exceptional at mathematics, English, and silence. Possesses "invisible competence" -- solving problems before they become visible. Patient, analytical, observant. Not driven by anger but by "an inheritance -- a question refined through a generation of silence into something harder and more precise than rage." Works at the Majestic Hotel front desk. Leaves Vietnam at nineteen with his father's list, the carved bird, and a notebook of names.
- **Ba Tam**: The woman who raised Minh. Small, leathery, precise. Sells lottery tickets. Counts money with surgical focus. Took Minh in because his father Khanh saved her during the war (Cu Chi). Lied about being Minh's aunt (she has no sister). Built an entire life around protecting Minh from his father's history. Practical even in grief. Navigates the neighborhood carefully -- always polite to the ward committee, always first to hang the flag.
- **Tran Van Khanh** (deceased): Minh's father. ARVN intelligence liaison between the South Vietnamese command and the Americans. Translated "not just language but intentions." Betrayed by Phong, missed the evacuation helicopter. Spent seven years in re-education camp Z30 near Xuan Loc (entered at 70kg, came out at 43kg). Carved a wooden bird from a roof beam with a nail, kept it five years. Attempted to reach America by boat in 1985 to find Phong -- the boat sank off Vung Tau. Drowned. Left a list of names with Uncle Bao.
- **Nguyen Thi Lan** (deceased): Minh's mother. Handed baby Minh over a fence to Ba Tam during the fall of Saigon. Died in a camp in Long Khanh province -- official record says malaria, 1976. "Beautiful and brave."
- **Dang Tuan Phong** (age ~57 in narrative present): The antagonist. Junior officer to Khanh. Told the Americans that Khanh was compromised (a lie), stole Khanh's seat on the evacuation helicopter. Now lives in Orange County -- owner of Saigon Plaza on Bolsa Avenue, respected community figure, generous donor to refugee resettlement programs. The symmetry of his philanthropy (helping refugees after stealing a refugee's escape) suggests either guilt dressed as charity or genuine compassion grown from betrayal.
- **Uncle Bao**: Former fellow soldier of Khanh. Thin, upright, with crooked fingers (broken in the re-education camp, healed without medical attention). Carries the burden of Khanh's story for decades. Provides Minh with the starting coordinates and Khanh's list. His hunger when looking at Minh suggests he has been waiting for someone young enough to act.
- **Colonel Vinh**: Named on Khanh's list. Rumored to be in Paris. Role in the betrayal unclear.
- **Mrs. Lieu**: Woman at the banh mi stand who first tells young Minh that Ba Tam has no sister.

## Story Rules
- **Genre**: Literary revenge thriller with strong historical grounding. Not a pulp action story -- the prose is contemplative, precise, and imagistic.
- **Narrative voice**: Third person, close to Minh. Lyrical but controlled. Long sentences that build like architecture, punctuated by short declarative statements.
- **POV**: Tightly limited to Minh's perspective and knowledge. Bao and Ba Tam provide information, but Minh must verify and interpret it himself.
- **Thematic complexity**: The novel refuses simple binaries. Phong may be "a monster or a mirror." Betrayal leaves scars on the betrayer as well as the betrayed. Khanh's note -- "The debt is not mine alone" -- suggests a conspiracy larger than one man. The story must maintain moral ambiguity.
- **Pacing**: Slow, deliberate. Information arrives in fragments over years. Minh inherits his father's patience and applies it as method. No shortcuts, no coincidences.
- **Avoid**: Do not reduce Phong to a cartoon villain. Do not have Minh act impulsively. Do not resolve the moral ambiguity of the situation -- the question of what Minh will do when he finds Phong should remain genuinely open. Do not lose the Vietnamese cultural specificity (language switching, food, family obligation, the architecture of lies).
- **Key motifs**: The carved wooden bird (prison art, love across silence). The photograph of two men -- one smiling, one not. The fence over which a mother handed her child. The list written in a dead man's hand. Maps -- incomplete, with blank edges dissolving into unknown territory.

## Plot Direction
- **Minh has left Vietnam** via Bangkok, heading ultimately toward Orange County. He carries the list, the bird, and his notebook.
- **Open threads**:
  - What will Minh do when he finds Phong? He does not know himself. Confession? Restitution? Something unnamed?
  - "The debt is not mine alone" -- who else participated in or profited from Khanh's betrayal? The list has names beyond Phong.
  - Colonel Vinh in Paris -- what does he know? What was his role?
  - The Saigon address at the top of the list (now a fabric warehouse) -- was there something hidden there?
  - Phong's possible complexity -- does he carry wounds from April 1975? Does he sleep well? What has his American life cost him internally?
  - Ba Tam's counter-narrative: "He chose his anger over his life. Over you." Will Minh repeat his father's path or find a different one?
  - The journey itself: Bangkok, then where? How does Minh cross borders, fund travel, build the capacity to find men who do not wish to be found?
