## Final Assessment

**Status: PASSED**

All dimensions >= 7, average score 8.625 >= 8.

**Strengths:**
- Authentic 2005 Taiwanese campus atmosphere with era-specific cultural details
- Ensemble cast with distinct voices, habits, and emotional arcs
- Natural blend of humor and emotional weight (九把刀-adjacent tone)
- Strong cliffhanger structure with Yang Siyuan's unopened note
- The friendship theme expressed through action (candy wrappers, corridor walks, sausage offerings) rather than exposition

**Key Characters:**
- **Lin Zhaoyang** (narrator): Everyman carrying his dead father's dream and his mother's expectations. Entry point for readers.
- **Zhao Xiangyu**: The sun — boundlessly generous, from Pingtung, candy wrapper collector. Hides family pain behind laughter.
- **Chen Bohan**: The analyst — left-handed, 45-degree tie, observes everything. Hiding from becoming his lawyer father.
- **Yang Siyuan**: The poet in the corner — onigiri, long sleeves, a secret. The catalytic figure whose crisis forces the others to grow.
- **Huang Junjie**: Literary society president — the writing mentor who teaches "truth" over technique.
- **Liao Meiling**: Homeroom teacher with the micro-smile — authority figure who respects intelligence.

**Collaboration Hooks:**
1. What is written on Yang Siyuan's folded note?
2. Will the narrator give Yang his letter?
3. What do Yang's long sleeves hide?
4. How do the midterm results change the group dynamic?
5. Will the literary society survive past 15 members?
6. Chen Bohan's inevitable confrontation with his father
7. Zhao Xiangyu's father — what happens when he "comes to Taipei personally"?
