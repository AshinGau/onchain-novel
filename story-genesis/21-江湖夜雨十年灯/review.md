**Round 1 Issues Identified:**
1. Opening needs a more distinctive hook — something aphoristic in the Gu Long style
2. Su Duhan's explanation in Ch2 is too clean; needs deliberate ambiguity so reader can't fully trust him
3. Atmosphere needs more specific sensory detail: lanterns, wine quality, seasonal details
4. The tomb's contents ("a military treatise") is generic — needs mystery about what's really inside
5. Voice needs more poetic economy and sharper metaphors

### Round 2: Atmosphere & Ambiguity Revision

**Changes Made:**
- Enhanced opening atmosphere: added lantern detail ("沈" character shadow swaying "like a drowning person"), wine description (rough corn liquor "like swallowing a rusty nail")
- Added vivid character details: tofu splitting into 8 exact pieces, instinct to check three death-points on people, old scholar saying his calligraphy "has killing intent"
- Introduced deliberate ambiguity for Su Duhan: internal monologue questioning whether his "no plan" is itself a plan; noting he says "七分是真的，剩下三分藏在眼神缝隙里"
- Su Duhan now twice evades the question of what's actually in the tomb ("那卷兵法——或者说那座墓里的东西")
- Enhanced metaphors: darkness as "a wide robe worn for ten years," sitting on stone "like frozen moonlight," stars appearing "like the first brave person standing up"
- Added farewell details in Ch3: the last character taught is "归" (return); eating hot tofu at the tofu shop

### Round 3: Hook & Originality Polish

**Changes Made:**
- Added aphoristic opening line: "这世上有两种人不该听雨：一种是正在赶路的人，一种是不再赶路的人" — immediately establishes the Gu Long-style voice and thematic concern
- Enhanced the silk scroll detail: the characters "兵策" appear to be written in lighter ink, as if the original words were scraped off and replaced — creating a mystery about what was originally written
- Added Shen's self-critique of his own choice: "这是一个懦夫的办法" and the three-month silent journey to Luoyan
- Enhanced the master's deathbed scene: he says "don't open" not "don't let it fall into wrong hands" — suggesting the tomb itself is dangerous, not just its contents
- Added the idea that the tomb may contain something "alive," not dead
- Connected the scattered clues: scraped-off scroll + master's fear + Su Duhan's evasions = a pattern the protagonist needs to assemble
- Added the fire metaphor about truth: "火焰的芯子是蓝色的，外面包着一层橙红，最外面是几乎看不见的透明"
- Added temple bells in final scene for atmospheric closure
- Added final lines establishing dual uncertainty: an unopenable tomb AND an untrustworthy companion

### Summary

The novel went through three rounds of revision:
- **Round 1** (avg 7.375): Solid foundation but generic in places, lacking distinctive atmosphere and voice
- **Round 2** (avg 7.875): Significantly improved atmosphere and character ambiguity; originality still lagging
- **Round 3** (avg 8.5): Distinctive opening, mystery deepened with the "alive tomb" concept and scraped scroll, voice fully realized

**Final verdict:** All 8 dimensions at 7 or above, average 8.5. The novel successfully combines the melancholic lone-swordsman aesthetic of Gu Long with Wen Ruian's visceral pacing, while introducing enough original mystery elements to sustain long-form collaboration.
