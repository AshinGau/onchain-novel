### Issues to Address
- Add "但方向反了" reversal to sharpen the opening hook
- Give Chen Yizhou prison-specific habits (scanning, seat selection) to differentiate him
- Show sister's personality through specific childhood anecdotes, not summary
- Give Zhou Dacheng a physical tic (glasses-pushing) and accounting-metaphor speech pattern
- Replace expository triangle explanation with more compressed, voice-driven prose
- Add sensory details to interrogation flashback (temperature, sound, milk tea)
- Vary sentence rhythm — mix short punches with longer passages
- Strengthen Ye Jiaming mystery with connection to prison guard

### Final Assessment
The genesis establishes a tightly controlled revenge narrative anchored in the physical and emotional geography of Mong Kok. The protagonist's twelve years in prison are not backstory but active presence — his habits, perceptions, and metaphors are all shaped by incarceration, giving the narration a distinctive voice that separates it from generic noir. The three chapters follow a clear dramatic arc: emotional foundation (Ch1: why), structural revelation (Ch2: who and how), and first action (Ch3: the opening move). The "我知" envelope strategy — psychological warfare rather than violence — establishes a Monte Cristo-grade intelligence in the protagonist without making him superhuman. The fusion of revenge and missing-person search creates a dual engine that will sustain long-form narrative. The Ye Jiaming mystery ensures that the conspiracy is not a simple triangle but a shape still being revealed. Co-authors inherit a world with clear rules (Mong Kok geography, power structures, character relationships) and genuine unknowns (Yilan's fate, the hidden evidence, the insider's motives) — fertile ground for continuation in any direction.
