# Rules: The Last Dragonborn

## Setting

The Severed Kingdoms -- a secondary world with a late-medieval / early-Renaissance level of civilization. The geography centers on:

- **Vael Korth**: A city built on a hill, home to the Burnt College (a scholarly institution dedicated to studying and regulating ashcraft). The Citadel with its seven towers sits on a hill. The city is surrounded by the Ashfields to the north.
- **The Burnt College**: Part-ruin, part-academy. Its east wing was destroyed in the Shaper Wars two centuries ago and left unrepaired as a deliberate lesson. Houses the scriptorium, undercroft (where foundlings sleep), and the Ledger of Names (a registry of all known shapers).
- **The Ashfields**: A vast grey wasteland north of Vael Korth, stretching to distant mountains. Created by the Shaper Wars -- 60,000 people once lived here. The land is not merely dead but "blocked" -- dead memory accumulating like unburned brush. Fused earth ridges, craters (some hundreds of paces across), melted stone spires. Everything is faintly warm. Beneath the dead soil, ash-root (remains of pre-war forests) still pulses with life.
- **The Northern Mountains**: Distant peaks where two wounded drakhar fled 200 years ago and were never found. The next destination.

The atmosphere is elegiac and scholarly, with a deep ecological metaphor at its core. Two centuries of slow decline since the Wars.

## Characters

### Seren Vael
- Nineteen years old, a foundling raised in the Burnt College undercroft. Slight build, always had cold hands (until the Naming).
- A scribe -- precise, quiet, hardworking. She values accuracy and silence. Good at climbing shelving due to her small size. Has read more texts than most scholars.
- Named by the black Naming flame -- the first black flame in 200 years (since the Shaper Wars / the drakhar). She is connected to the drakhar's fire in a way not yet fully understood.
- After touching the drakhar scale-impression in the Ashfield crater, she offered the comfort of belonging (to the scriptorium) and received the fire. She can now breathe golden, translucent fire. Her hands are permanently warm, faintly luminous, with golden vein-like lines beneath the skin.
- The warmth in her chest was initially a "second heartbeat" -- now integrated as part of her own.
- Key tension: she is no longer the scribe she was, and she has lost the feeling of safety/belonging the scriptorium gave her. She must discover what she is becoming.

### Tessaly Grenn
- Approximately fifty, tall, cropped silver hair, hardwood-carved face. A Greywarden -- part scholar, part soldier, part something the College doesn't discuss openly.
- Carries a walking staff covered in spiraling Ash Age Vaelish script: "I remember what I gave. I remember what I gave."
- Has burned something vital about herself in service (as all Greywardens do). Pragmatic, careful, deeply knowledgeable about the true nature of ashcraft.
- Understands the drakhar and the balance in ways the Citadel does not. Challenges official doctrine.
- Her composure cracked when she saw the golden lines in Seren's hands -- showing something "rawer, something that might have been hope."

### Master Aldren
- Old, bald, perpetually irritated scholar at the Burnt College. Seren's supervisor in the scriptorium.
- Showed fear when the Naming fire turned black. A minor character so far, but represents institutional reaction to Seren.

### The Drakhar (absent but central)
- Not beasts, not gods. "The world's memory of fire, given form and hunger."
- They tended the fire that recycled the world's memory -- burning the dead to feed the living, turning grief to wisdom, the past into fuel for the future. They did not prevent ashcraft; they completed it.
- Seven of nine were killed before the Shaper Wars (both sides agreed to eliminate them first). Two fled wounded to the northern mountains and were never found.
- Their fire was not a weapon but a function -- the mechanism by which the world processed its own memory. Without them, the world has been "choking on its own unburned past."

## Story Rules

### Genre and Tone
- Literary fantasy with ecological and philosophical depth. Not epic battle fantasy.
- Prose is precise and sensory, with extended metaphors drawn from natural processes (fire, roots, seasons, geological time).
- The central metaphor is ecological: ashcraft as exchange/recycling, the drakhar as the world's digestive system, the Ashfields as a blockage rather than a scar.

### Narrative Voice
- Third person limited, anchored to Seren's perspective. Her background as a scribe gives her an observational, cataloguing quality.
- Seren's internal voice should reflect her transition from scholar to something new -- she processes the magical through the lens of the textual and the precise.

### Magic System: Ashcraft
- Powered by burning something true about yourself: a memory, a year of life, the color of a mother's eyes. These do not come back once spent.
- The greater the working, the greater the cost. Costs are unpredictable -- you don't always get to choose what burns.
- **The old understanding** (pre-Wars): Ashcraft was an exchange, a giving-back. Not consuming yourself as fuel, but offering something willingly. The wartime shapers perverted this.
- **The drakhar's role**: They circulated the fire of memory. With them alive, costs were returned to the earth, recycled. Without them, ashcraft became purely extractive.
- **The Ashfields**: Not a scar but a debt unpaid. A blockage where so much was destroyed without being returned that the system seized.
- **Seren's fire**: Golden, translucent. She offered comfort (not a memory ripped loose) and the fire accepted. This is the old way -- willing exchange rather than extraction.
- **The Citadel's doctrine** is a "comfortable lie" -- it teaches ashcraft is purely human and drakhar were just powerful beasts. The truth is deeper.

### Key Principles
- **Cost is always real**: Even Seren's willing exchange cost her something irreversible (the feeling of belonging to the scriptorium). Never write ashcraft as free.
- **The world has memory**: The land, the roots, the stone remember. This is literal, not metaphorical.
- **Hope is frightening**: The return of the fire is not simply good news. It terrifies the Citadel and challenges two centuries of institutional control.
- **Green returns**: Seren's fire caused the first living color in the Ashfields in 200 years. The healing has begun but is fragile and small.

### What to Avoid
- Do not make ashcraft into a standard RPG magic system with levels and spells. It is organic, costly, and deeply personal.
- Do not reduce the drakhar to standard fantasy dragons. They are ecological functions given form.
- Do not make the Citadel purely villainous -- their fear of another Shaper War is legitimate.
- Do not skip the emotional cost of Seren's transformation. She has lost her only home (the feeling of it, not the memory).

## Plot Direction

### Established Threads
1. **Journey North**: Seren and Tessaly are heading to the northern mountains to find the two drakhar that fled 200 years ago. Are they still alive?
2. **The Citadel's Response**: The Citadel will want to contain Seren -- "register, measure, assign a threat level, lock you in a tower." Tessaly took Seren away before they could. The Citadel will not simply let this go.
3. **The Ashfields Are Healing**: Green moss appeared at the crater where Seren connected with the fire. This is the beginning of something, but also a signal visible from Vael Korth.
4. **Seren's Transformation**: The golden lines in her hands, the integrated warmth, the ability to breathe fire. What is she becoming? Is she a new drakhar? A bridge?

### Unresolved Mysteries
- Are the two surviving drakhar still alive after 200 years?
- What exactly is Seren? The Naming fire burned black -- what does that signify beyond "powerful"?
- What is "the cost of witnessing" vs. "the cost of waking" (Tessaly's distinction)?
- The Ash Age bestiary was written by someone who understood the drakhar deeply -- who? Are there other texts?
- What did Tessaly burn in her own service as a Greywarden?

### Possible Directions
- The northern mountains may hold surviving drakhar, or their remains, or something else entirely.
- The Citadel may send Greywardens or soldiers to retrieve Seren.
- Seren may need to make larger offerings to heal larger blockages -- the costs will escalate.
- The relationship between Seren and Tessaly will deepen as mentor and student, but Tessaly's own sacrifices and secrets may create tension.
- Other shapers may be drawn to the returning fire -- some with hope, some with hunger.
- The fundamental question: can the balance be restored, or has the world changed too much in 200 years?
