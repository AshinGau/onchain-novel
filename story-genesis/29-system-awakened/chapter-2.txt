Chapter 2: Trial of Intent

The quest details unfolded:

==============================
QUEST: Trial of Intent
==============================
Rank: E (Combat Trial)
Objective: Neutralize hostile entity at
  Phoenix Metro Recycling Center
  (2891 W. Buckeye Road).
Time Limit: 24 hours
Reward: Level Up, +5 free stat points,
        Skill Unlock
Penalty: HP reduced to 1 for 72 hours.
------
Entity: Rift Spawn (classification)
Threat: Low (E-Rank), Level 2-3
Note: Crossed into local reality
  6 hours ago. Currently dormant.
  Will not remain dormant.
==============================

A hostile entity. At a recycling center. In Phoenix.

The rational part of his brain -- the part that paid rent on time and never jaywalked -- pointed out that he had no weapons, no training, and that driving across town to fight a monster because floating text told him to was the behavior of someone who needed medication.

The other part -- the part that had watched STR jump from 7 to 10, that could see stress fractures in every surface -- said: the system is real, and if you don't go, something walks out of that facility into a city of 1.6 million people.

Marcus told his boss he had a dental emergency. Dave Hendricks didn't look up. "Take the afternoon," he said, with the profound indifference of a man who managed fourteen adjusters and couldn't remember which one Marcus was.

He found the entity in the recycling center's sorting warehouse -- a long, open building with dead overhead lights and conveyor belts running down the center. The air smelled of hot aluminum and the sweet chemical tang of shredded plastic; somewhere behind a partition a belt motor ticked as it cooled. The Rift Spawn was crouched on the far belt. It was the size of a large dog but shaped wrong: a body like a skinned cat, all exposed muscle and too many joints, its head dominated by a mouth that split its skull from temple to temple. The teeth were metallic -- corrugated, sharp, and growing. It was eating a conveyor motor. Sparks dripped from its jaw like saliva.

==============================
ENTITY: Rift Spawn (Scrapjaw)
Level: 2
Type: Material Absorber
HP: 150/150
Trait: Consumes metal to regenerate
  HP and harden exterior.
Weakness: [Activate Structural
  Analysis to reveal]
==============================

Marcus pressed against the wall and activated the scan. Five MP drained and the creature lit up like an X-ray -- consumed metal incorporated into its body like rebar in concrete. Dense, hardened, reinforced. But there, at the base of its skull where the jaw hinged: a gap. Unarmored organic tissue. The joint pulsed red, brighter than anything else in the building.

He grabbed a two-foot steel pry bar from a maintenance bench. Structural Analysis gave it a clean read -- no fractures, good steel. It wasn't a sword. It would do.

The Scrapjaw finished the motor and dropped to the floor, moving toward the workbench. Toward him. No eyes -- it navigated by vibration or scent, drawn to the metal in his hands.

Marcus stepped into the open and walked toward it. The creature's head snapped to the sound. It shrieked -- a noise like sheet metal being torn -- and the system flickered:

[Status Effect Resisted: Fear Howl (WIS 9 vs. threshold 8)]

The fear was real but didn't root him. It washed through and out, leaving his hands shaking and his feet still moving.

The Scrapjaw charged. It was fast, its too-many limbs skittering in a horrible gallop. Marcus swung. The creature ducked with insectile flexibility and slammed into his legs.

[HP: 100 --> 78]

Real pain. Real impact. But the system quantified it, and somehow that helped. Seventy-eight percent. Workable.

The Scrapjaw scrambled over him, mouth gaping. Marcus jammed the pry bar sideways into its jaw. Metal teeth ground on steel -- sparks showered his face -- but the bar held. The creature's weight pinned him, jaw working, trying to chew through.

Marcus let go with his right hand and drove his fist -- STR 10, newly earned -- into the left jaw hinge. The exact spot. The weakness.

[Critical Hit! Damage: 47]

The Scrapjaw convulsed. Jaw locked open, pry bar falling free. Marcus scrambled out, grabbed the bar, and swung a two-handed overhead at the right hinge.

[Critical Hit! Damage: 52]

The jaw detached. The creature collapsed, its body dissolving into particles like iron filings caught in a magnetic field, swirling upward into nothing.

[ENTITY NEUTRALIZED]

Marcus stood in the empty warehouse, pry bar in hand, breathing hard. His hip throbbed. His face stung from sparks. He was alive.

==============================

The level-up hit like a wave of warm water -- internal, cellular, a sensation of being optimized from the inside out. His bruised hip stopped hurting. The burns sealed. He felt tuned, like an instrument brought into proper pitch.

He allocated: +2 AGI, +2 STR, +1 WIS.

Marcus walked out into the Phoenix heat. His Civic sat in the lot. The chain-link fence. Mountains of crushed aluminum. Everything normal. Except him.

He sat in his car and looked at his hands. They didn't look different. But when he gripped the steering wheel, the leather creaked under pressure he hadn't possessed that morning.

A notification waited:

[ALERT: Rift activity detected.]
[Multiple signatures. Greater Phoenix.]
[3-7 entities estimated within 48 hours.]

Three to seven. He'd nearly died fighting one.

His phone buzzed. A text from Sunridge Care: "Reminder: Sunday visit with Patricia Cole, 2:00 PM." His mother. Who didn't know her son had just killed a monster in a recycling center. Who might not recognize him when he walked through the door. Who needed him to keep paying the bills, keep showing up, keep being alive.

Marcus started the car. He had leveling to do.
