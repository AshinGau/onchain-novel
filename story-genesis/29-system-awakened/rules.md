# Rules: System Awakened

## Setting
Contemporary Phoenix, Arizona, USA. Summer (June). Key locations: Hendricks & Associates Insurance office (Marcus's day job), Phoenix Metro Recycling Center (first combat), South Mountain Park (rift breach, multi-target combat), Marcus's apartment (parking garage training), Sunridge Care facility (mother's nursing home). The city's desert landscape -- creosote bushes, saguaro cacti, flash-flood washes, crushing summer heat, mountains of crushed aluminum -- serves as the arena. The world is modern and mundane on the surface, but rifts are beginning to tear through reality, spawning hostile entities. Authorities are starting to notice ("unexplained sinkholes," Phoenix PD investigating). The system is invisible to non-hosts.

## Characters
- **Marcus Cole** -- 28 years old. Insurance adjuster at Hendricks & Associates. Lean from skipping meals, not discipline. Innate pattern recognition -- always heard the 60hz buzz in fluorescent lights, always sensed where numbers were wrong in spreadsheets, always almost-noticed stress fractures in buildings. This latent ability is why the system selected him (Compatibility Index: 97.3%). His mother Patricia had a stroke and is in Sunridge Care; he visits every Sunday, she recognizes him about half the time. Her care bills are a constant financial pressure. Personality: practical, analytical, quietly determined. Not brave by nature but willing to act when stakes are clear. Allocates stat points strategically (favoring AGI and STR). Currently Level 4.
- **Patricia Cole** -- Marcus's mother. In Sunridge Care facility after a stroke. Sometimes doesn't recognize him. The "Cole family build" -- "made for thinking, not lifting." Her care bills are Marcus's primary motivation.
- **Priya** -- Marcus's cubicle neighbor at the insurance office. Serves as a reminder of his normal life. Cannot see the system or its effects.
- **Dave Hendricks** -- Marcus's boss. Profoundly indifferent. Manages fourteen adjusters and can't remember which one Marcus is.
- **Unknown Host** -- Detected by System Sense at 487 meters northwest on South Mountain during the rift breach quest. Level unknown, disposition unknown. Was watching Marcus fight the entire time. Has not made contact. First indication that Marcus is not alone.

## Story Rules
- **Genre**: LitRPG / urban fantasy / power progression. The system interface (stat screens, quest panels, damage numbers) is presented in monospaced text blocks. Combat is tactical and grounded -- weaknesses must be discovered through Structural Analysis, exploited through positioning and terrain, not through raw power.
- **Narrative voice**: Close third-person, Marcus's POV. Dry, observational, slightly sardonic. The prose contrasts the mundane (insurance spreadsheets, bad coffee, IKEA furniture) with the extraordinary (monsters, stat screens, gold light). This contrast is the story's tonal signature.
- **System mechanics**: The system is real, quantified, and consequential. Stats, HP, MP, skills, quests, rewards, and penalties are all concrete. Penalties are harsh -- permanent stat reductions, HP reduction to 1 for 72 hours. The system does not coddle. It "observes" and "does not choose sides."
- **Combat design**: Every fight must involve Structural Analysis identifying specific weak points. Marcus wins through precision and environmental awareness, not brute force. He uses improvised weapons (pry bars, rebar) -- he does not have access to magical weapons yet. Damage numbers and HP tracking must be consistent.
- **Progression pacing**: Level-ups feel meaningful and physical ("warm water wave, every cell vibrating"). Each level brings tangible, described improvement. Stat allocation should reflect Marcus's analytical personality.
- **Avoid**: Overpowered protagonist -- Marcus should struggle, take real damage, and face genuine risk. Video game humor or fourth-wall breaking. Making the system benevolent -- it has motives, it punishes failure, it is an entity with its own agenda. Ignoring the mundane life -- Marcus still has a job, bills, a sick mother. The tension between his normal obligations and his system responsibilities is essential.

## Plot Direction
- **The other host**: System Sense detected someone on South Mountain watching the fight. "Some hosts build. Some hosts break." This is the primary narrative tension going forward -- who is this person, what is their level, and are they friend or foe?
- **Escalating rifts**: 3-7 entities estimated in greater Phoenix within 48 hours. The threat is accelerating. Marcus is Level 4 and nearly died fighting one Level 2 creature in chapter 2. The power gap demands rapid leveling.
- **Public awareness**: News reports are connecting the anomalies ("unexplained sinkhole," "second anomaly near South Mountain"). The world is starting to notice. How long before rifts and entities become public knowledge?
- **Marcus's dual life**: He still works at the insurance office. His mother still needs care. The system is pulling him into a role that will eventually be incompatible with his normal life.
- **System mysteries**: Why was Marcus selected? What is on the other side of the rifts? What does the system want? "The system observes" -- but to what end? The system showed "hesitation" (flickering text) when revealing other hosts exist. It has emotional tells.
- **Skill development**: Structural Analysis (Lv.2) is Marcus's signature ability. Danger Sense (Lv.1) and System Sense (Lv.1) are new. Future skill unlocks should build on his pattern-recognition foundation. The "abstract structures" mentioned in Structural Analysis's description hint that the skill could eventually analyze systems, organizations, or even other hosts.
- **Stat snapshot**: STR 15 / AGI 12 / VIT 10 / INT 12 / WIS 11 / LUK 5. LUK is conspicuously low and has never been invested in. This may become relevant.
