## Final Assessment

**Status: PASSED**

All dimensions >= 8. Average score = 8.375 (threshold: 8.0).

### Strengths
- **Moral ambiguity** elevates the revenge genre: Sun Shouyi is not a simple villain — the mantou scene and the grandfather-holding-grandson moment create genuine moral complexity
- **Multi-generational character design**: three Zhao men, three different responses to injustice (silence / reckless courage / methodical pursuit)
- **Authentic 眷村 atmosphere**: specific foods, dialects, communal living, nostalgia for irreproducible home flavors — grounded in the literary tradition of Bai Xianyong and Long Yingtai
- **Engineering-as-metaphor**: Zhengyang's programmer mindset (TODO.md, encrypted folders, "this operation may be irreversible") creates a distinctive modern voice for generational revenge
- **The iron box** as a recurring physical symbol: rust accumulates as years pass, moved through three homes, unlocked only at death

### Key Unresolved Threads for Co-Authors
1. **The 1949 Murder** — Who did Sun Shouyi kill on the Qingdao dock? Was it the 连长? Another soldier? A civilian?
2. **The Missing Five Days** — Sept 18-22, 1991: What did Zhao Jianguo discover between his last notebook entry and his death?
3. **The Phone Call** — Who called Zhao Jianguo at 10 PM on the night he died?
4. **The Threatening Text** — Who sent the 2 AM message to Zhao Zhengyang? Sun Weizhe's people? Or Sun Zhiheng himself?
5. **Sun Weizhe** — Is the second-generation chairman aware of his father's 1949 crimes and 1991 cover-up? Is he a co-conspirator or an unknowing inheritor?
6. **Chen Shuide's Secret** — He "also got some things" in 1949 — what did he receive? Does his paralyzed silence conceal more?
7. **The 1973 Trip** — Zhao Dechang kept this secret from everyone, including his grandson. Will Zhengyang discover it? How will it change his understanding?
8. **The Boy in Red** — The 5-6 year old grandson Sun Zhiheng held in 1973 — is he Sun Weizhe? What does he know?
9. **The Kaohsiung Port Redevelopment** — Hengyi Group's 18-billion-dollar project at the same port where Zhao Jianguo died. Coincidence or cover-up?

### Style Notes
- Narrative voice: Third-person close, alternating between Zhao Dechang (past) and Zhao Zhengyang (present)
- Tense: Past tense throughout, with present-tense internal reflection for Zhengyang
- Language: Mandarin Chinese with dialect-inflected dialogue (山东腔、河南腔、安徽话) for 眷村 authenticity
- Tone: Restrained emotional intensity — grief and anger are contained, never melodramatic. The loudest moments are the quietest (Dechang sitting in silence, Dechang walking away from Sun in Kaohsiung).
