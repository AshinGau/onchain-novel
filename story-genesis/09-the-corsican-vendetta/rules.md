# Rules: The Corsican Vendetta

## Setting
- **Time**: Contemporary Corsica (early 2020s). The vendetta between the Ferracci and Lucchesi families spans forty years, rooted in a 1981 shooting.
- **Primary locations**: Cozzano (mountain village in Corsica's interior -- granite, chestnut forests, the church of San Ghjuvanni with its cracked bell). Ajaccio (the capital -- Route des Sanguinaires, Cours Napoleon, Rue Fesch, the port). Corte (citadel town at Corsica's center, Paoli's capital). Bastia (northeastern port city where Anto works). The maquis -- dense scrubland of cistus, myrtle, wild mint, rosemary.
- **Atmosphere**: An island governed by two systems simultaneously -- the visible French bureaucracy and the invisible Corsican code beneath it. "Roots beneath asphalt." Stone buildings, chestnut timber, terrain so rugged a man can vanish. The vendetta is "not a custom but a grammar."
- **Social context**: Corsican identity exists in tension with French governance. The old codes (vendetta, family honor, debt systems) operate beneath modernity. The gendarmes are French; the matters are Corsican.

## Characters
- **Anto Ferracci** (31): Protagonist. Dark, angular face -- Ferracci cheekbones, shoulders held square and slightly forward "as though preparing to walk into wind." Works at the port authority in Bastia. Former competitive archer (callus on left index finger from bowstring -- he runs his thumb along it when thinking). Methodical, patient, controlled. His grief manifests as calculation, not tears. Carries his father's Beretta 92FS. His mother's condition for the vendetta: come back alive.
- **Petru Ferracci** (deceased, 67): Anto's father. Shepherd who was "something more." Carried a Beretta for forty years, used it twice -- once on a dog, once to shoot Santu Lucchesi in the thigh in 1981 (a warning, not a murder). Found in his bergerie with throat cut ear to ear. His dog Orsu killed with a single blow. The knife was a message: "I was close enough to smell your breath."
- **Livia Ferracci** (58): Anto's mother. Born Sansonetti of Bastelica. Tall, carries grief in her spine (pulled upward, not down). Has not spoken since the body was brought from the bergerie. Gives Anto the vendetta's condition: "I am not telling you to forget. I am telling you to survive."
- **Santu Lucchesi** (72): Patriarch of the Lucchesi family. Lives in villa "A Torra" on the Route des Sanguinaires. Shot in the thigh by Petru Ferracci in 1981. "Waited forty years" -- patience as weapon. Finds adequacy in most things, which makes him dangerous. Controls a web of political debts -- "the island's central bank for thirty years."
- **Petru-Santu Lucchesi** (45): Eldest son. Runs Batisseurs du Sud, the largest construction firm in southern Corsica. Municipal councilor. Wears linen suits, speaks continental French. Legitimate by external measure. Depends on government contracts secured through the Corsican debt system. Anto assesses him: "a politician who would not risk blood on a contract."
- **Dume Lucchesi** (41): Second son. Manages bars, nightclubs, restaurants in Ajaccio and Porto-Vecchio. Cannabis distribution through contacts in Marseille. "Controversial" in newspapers that rarely mention him. Anto assesses him: no motive for the murder.
- **Lisandru Lucchesi** (33): Third son. The most dangerous. Five years in the Foreign Legion (Second REP, paratroopers, Calvi). No official employment, no social media, no photographs. "His father's knife." Moves quietly, has military discipline -- systematic eye-sweeping of spaces, controlled blankness. Not married, no children, no attachments to exploit. "Exists the way smoke exists." In the confrontation at Corte, denied killing Petru but used suspicious grammar: accounted for every Lucchesi except himself as the one who ordered rather than acted.
- **Marcu Ferracci**: Anto's cousin. Mechanic in Ajaccio. Drives a battered Peugeot. Reluctant but useful -- conducts surveillance on Vannina Lucchesi's routine. Voices caution: "Women are not part of this."
- **Matteu Ferracci**: Distant cousin. Bar owner on Rue Fesch in Ajaccio. Sixty years old, face like a dried fig, hands trembling from Parkinson's. Architectural memory of every transaction, debt, and alliance in Ajaccio for forty years. Provides Anto the map of the Lucchesi empire.
- **Father Benedettu**: Village priest who baptized Anto, married his parents, and will likely bury more Ferraccis.
- **Vannina Lucchesi**: Petru-Santu's wife. Precise routine: mass Sunday, hairdresser Tuesday, lunch with sister Thursday. Target of Marcu's surveillance -- not to be harmed but studied as a window into her husband.

## Story Rules
- **Genre**: Literary vendetta thriller grounded in Corsican culture and landscape. Not a crime procedural -- the prose has the weight of landscape writing and anthropological observation.
- **Narrative voice**: Third person, close to Anto. Sentences are long, cadenced, often structured around geographic and sensory detail. The landscape is a character -- granite, maquis, chestnut forests, the scops owl's patient call.
- **Language**: Corsican is the language of the dead, of family, of vendetta. French is the language of bureaucracy and outsiders. Latin is for God. This linguistic layering must be maintained.
- **POV**: Limited to Anto's perspective. He builds his understanding incrementally through observation, conversation, and inference.
- **Vendetta grammar**: The vendetta has rules -- it is "a grammar, organizing the relationship between injury and response, between the living and the dead." Injury requires response. The family is the hand, not the individual. A knife across the throat is a message; a gunshot is efficiency. These codes must be respected even by characters who operate outside them.
- **Pacing**: Deliberate and patient. Anto moves like a man navigating by stars -- "patience, a tolerance for darkness, and the ability to hold a destination in mind long after it has vanished from sight." Action scenes are brief and weighted, not extended.
- **Avoid**: Do not turn this into a generic thriller. The Corsican specificity (food, landscape, dialect, social codes) is essential. Do not make the Lucchesis incompetent -- they are a powerful, intelligent family. Do not resolve whether Lisandru killed Petru too quickly -- the ambiguity is the engine. Do not lose the mother's voice as moral counterweight.
- **Key motifs**: The cracked church bell. The Beretta as "a second spine." The bowstring callus. Oleanders (beautiful and poisonous). Stone and knife as opposing forces (patience vs. violence). Navigation by stars.

## Plot Direction
- **The confrontation at Corte has occurred.** Anto and Lisandru have met face to face. Lisandru denied the killing but revealed suspicious knowledge. Both men now know the other is watching.
- **Open threads**:
  - Did Lisandru kill Petru? His denial had a grammatical gap -- he never denied ordering it. His use of "tragedy" (rather than "shame" or "unfortunate") implies understanding of the mechanics.
  - What was the full history of the land dispute? A chestnut grove, a moved stone marker, dead goats -- then blood. Is there more beneath the surface?
  - The Lucchesi empire's vulnerabilities: Petru-Santu's dependence on political relationships (discretion-dependent), Dume's maritime supply chain (interruptible), Lisandru's invisibility (can be made visible). Which joint will Anto target?
  - Marcu's surveillance of Vannina -- what will it reveal about Petru-Santu's weak points?
  - The figure on the ridge watching Anto drive from Corte -- shepherd, hunter, or Lucchesi sentinel?
  - Anto has three days of leave remaining. After that, he returns to Bastia. How does he continue the vendetta while maintaining his civilian life?
  - Livia's condition: survive. Can Anto pursue vengeance and survive? Is there a path that satisfies both the living and the dead?
