### Issues to Address
- Ch2 (11,139 chars) and Ch3 (12,160 chars) exceed 10,000-character limit -- must trim
- Anto needs distinctive personal traits beyond his avenger role (habits, physical tics, internal contradictions)
- "The way..." simile structure overused -- reduce to 3-4 instances maximum
- Livia needs a moment of agency, not just silent mourning
- Ch2 exposition too briefing-like; needs more narrative movement
- Add moral complexity -- doubt, internal resistance, the cost of vendetta
- Originality: need to subvert revenge genre expectations somewhere

### Final Assessment
The Corsican Vendetta delivers a strong genesis for collaborative continuation. The central premise -- a blood feud between mountain families, rooted in a disputed chestnut grove and escalated across forty years -- is grounded in real Corsican vendetta tradition rather than generic revenge narrative. The three chapters form a clean arc: funeral and oath (establishing emotional justification), intelligence gathering (revealing the enemy's power), and first confrontation (the tense cafe scene in Corte). The story's signature device -- vendetta as grammar, with its semicolons, periods, and tells -- gives the prose a distinctive intellectual dimension without sacrificing emotional weight. Livia's terrace scene provides the moral complexity the genre demands: the vendetta is neither glorified nor condemned but presented as an inheritance, like the land itself. The ambiguity about Lisandru's guilt (the gap in his denial, the "tragedy" word choice) gives co-authors genuine mystery to work with, not just action to continue. The Corsican setting is immersive and specific -- maquis ecology, village culture, the French/Corsican linguistic divide, food, architecture, honor codes. Ready for publication.
