### Round 2: Conflict, Face-Slapping & Sensory Revision

**Changes Made:**
- **Ch1**: Added face-slapping confrontation with landlord 肥佬黎 (fat landlord Lai) who tries to raise rent and looks down on the protagonist. Chen prophesies his downfall. Added memory-doubt sequence for lottery numbers, creating genuine tension about whether they're correct.
- **Ch2**: Added full face-slapping scene with 周锦年 (Zhou Jinnian), arrogant senior from Peregrine Securities who mocks the put-warrant strategy. Dramatic irony: reader knows Peregrine will collapse in Jan 1998. Enhanced crash scene with visceral sensory details: mascara tears, smell of fear ("iron rust and sour milk"), elderly bus driver crying on phone.
- **Ch3**: Added 何兆丰 (He Zhaofeng) as an antagonist — a veteran property developer who tries to outbid Chen for the 168 building. Chen faces him down in a tea house confrontation using superior market knowledge. Fixed garbled characters (金钟). Trimmed ending for tighter impact.
- Gave Leung Pak-To distinctive trait: red pen for horse racing, blue pen for trade slips, never mixed up.

### Round 3: Vulnerability, Existential Stakes & Mystery Element

**Changes Made:**
- **Ch1**: Added post-rebirth existential crisis — protagonist experiences lingering death-memory, realizes rebirth might be "a loan with a deadline" (一张有时限的借条). Creates genuine uncertainty: will his body remember it's supposed to die? Adds vulnerability and time-pressure stakes that standard rebirth novels lack.
- **Ch2**: Added mysterious old man at Star Ferry pier — someone who watches the stock crash with the same unnatural calm as the protagonist. Seeds the possibility that Chen Yuanming is not the only reborn person. This is a major originality boost: introduces potential competition/threat from another time-traveler, and creates a compelling mystery thread.
- Refined the ending of Ch3: sister falling asleep on his shoulder, mother's dishwashing sounds as rhythm — more intimate and emotionally resonant. Compressed the timeline exposition into fewer lines for tighter impact.

## Summary

Three rounds of revision transformed a standard rebirth power fantasy into a more distinctive work with:
- **Existential stakes**: rebirth is not free — it may have a time limit
- **Genuine vulnerability**: the protagonist carries the memory of his own death
- **Mystery element**: a possible rival reborn person
- **Escalating face-slapping**: from petty landlord to financial elite to property tycoon
- **Emotional core**: mother's "你食咗饭未" as the constant amid all the upheaval
- **Authentic period atmosphere**: deeply researched 1997 Hong Kong handover setting

The three chapters establish a solid foundation for a long-running serial: Ch1 (rebirth + first move), Ch2 (first fortune via financial crisis), Ch3 (empire foundation + team assembly). Multiple threads are left open for continuation.
