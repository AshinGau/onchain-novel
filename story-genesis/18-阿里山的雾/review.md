# 阿里山的雾 - Genesis Review

## Story Summary
- **Narrator**: Fang Qiming (方启明), freelance writer on assignment for an outdoor magazine
- **Core Horror**: A hiking team of seven gets lost in supernatural fog on Alishan. They discover a Japanese colonial-era shrine (久安鎮魂社) that was built not to pacify human spirits, but to contain the souls of hundreds of thousands of cypress trees felled during the colonial logging era. The trees are reclaiming bodies — turning people into trees.
- **Techniques Used**: Found manuscript framing, accumulating anomalies (GPS failure, missing footprints, circular paths), body horror (human-to-tree transformation), folk horror (Tsou indigenous knowledge vs. colonial legacy), isolation horror (fog as prison), group dynamics under extreme stress

## Open Threads for Co-Authors

1. **The West Wall** — What painting was covered with white pigment? The crack reveals a vertical black shape identical to the figure the narrator saw in the fog.
2. **Abus's Fate** — He vanished outside the shrine, not inside. The tree at his location was only 3 years old. Did the red thread protect him? Did he escape through Tsou knowledge?
3. **The Red Thread** — Abus gave everyone red thread. Only the narrator and Boan kept theirs. Does it actually work? Why did Fangyi lose hers?
4. **The Pit** — What is at the bottom? It breathes, it burned Abus's hand, its floor rises. The voices from within speak Tsou, Japanese, and an unknown language.
5. **The Stone Tablets** — Rows of tombstone-like markers with tree-ring patterns instead of names. The smallest has only three rings. Are these markers for transformed humans?
6. **Boan and Fangyi** — A pregnant woman and her husband, each wearing a red thread. Will they escape? What happens to the unborn child in a place that transforms humans into trees?
7. **Zonghan's Notebooks** — He kept three notebooks. He voluntarily transformed. Are his research notes still readable? Do they contain insights the narrator missed?
8. **The Narrator's Year-Ring** — One ring appeared on his palm. He has no red thread. How long does he have?
9. **The Approaching Root-Steps** — At the end, hundreds of thousands of root-systems converge on the shrine. What happens when they arrive?
10. **The Shrine's Builders** — Who commissioned a shrine of this scale at this elevation? Why "久安" (eternal peace)? Why was it abandoned — or was it?
