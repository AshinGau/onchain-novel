## Summary

Three chapters (~20,000 characters total) establishing a modern-day Taipei urban fantasy. The story follows Lin Zhaoyang, a physics graduate student at National Taiwan University, whose spirit eye (灵眼) is forcibly awakened by a 200-year-old banyan tree on Ghost Month night. He apprentices under Zhang Daoxuan, a reclusive Daoist master of the Lüshan (闾山) tradition operating from a private altar in Wanhua, Taipei's oldest district. Key innovations include: the "spirit eye as frequency dial" metaphor with learnable control; the talisman system presented through concrete failure and success rather than exposition; the "seal the source, don't chase the symptom" (封穴 vs 驱鬼) approach to spiritual threats; and the mysterious "敕" mark on Lin's wrist whose origin even his master cannot explain. The story blends modern Taipei life (PTT, MRT, night markets, 全聯超市) with traditional Taiwanese folk religion (宮廟文化, 閭山派符法, 掲筊, 環香) in a way that grounds the supernatural in specific, recognizable urban texture. Multiple threads are open for continuation: the wrist mark's origin, additional 地脈 threats beneath Taipei, the incoming 北投 commission, and Zhang Daoxuan's unspoken past.
